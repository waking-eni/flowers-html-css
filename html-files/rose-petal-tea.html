The Beauty of Flower Tea

Rose Petal Tea

The best roses to use for teas are highly fragrant and tend to be red and pink old-fashioned varieties. The tea generally tastes like the roseâ€™s fragrance, so the taste can vary depending on the roses used. Most have a floral, tangy taste. Before using the fresh petals, rinse them off and cut off the white bases (they are bitter). To make the tea, simmer two cups of fresh rose petals in three cups of water for about five minutes, then strain out the liquid. Add honey, sugar, or sweetener if you please. This tea is full of vitamin C, and it smells heavenly!

Hibiscus Tea

Hibiscus tea is popular in Jamaica and the Caribbean and is generally made from Hibiscus sabdariffa. There are a variety of hibiscus tea recipes. The simplest recipe is to pour boiling water over two tablespoons of fresh, chopped hibiscus flowers, steep for five minutes, and strain. Sweeten to taste. Dried hibiscus flowers can be used as well, but only a teaspoon is needed. Red flowers make a beautiful bright-red tea. Try serving it iced with sugar, orange slices, or lime juice. Hibiscus tea has a tart, lemony taste that I love!
